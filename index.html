1. Types of Hotels

Luxury Hotels:

These hotels offer the highest level of comfort, luxury, and personalized service. They often feature spacious rooms or suites, upscale amenities, fine dining restaurants, spas, fitness centers, and other lavish facilities. Luxury hotels are known for their attention to detail and strive to provide a memorable and indulgent experience.

Business Hotels:

Business Hotel primarily caters to corporate travelers and provides convenient amenities for their needs. They typically have well-equipped meeting rooms, conference facilities, high-speed internet access, business centers, and executive lounges. Business hotels are strategically located near business districts or major transportation hubs to facilitate easy travel for business travelers.

Resort Hotels:

Resort hotels are designed for leisure and relaxation. They are often situated in scenic locations, such as beachfronts, mountains, or lush landscapes. Resort hotels typically offer a wide range of recreational facilities like swimming pools, spas, sports facilities, entertainment options, and multiple dining venues. They aim to provide a complete vacation experience within the hotel premises.

2. Cuisine

Italian Cuisine:

Italian cuisine is known for its simplicity, fresh ingredients, and bold flavors. It includes dishes like pasta, pizza, risotto, antipasti, gelato, and tiramisu.

French Cuisine:

French cuisine is renowned for its sophisticated techniques and delicate flavors. It features dishes such as coq au vin, bouillabaisse, escargots, croissants, and crème brûlée.

Chinese Cuisine:

Chinese cuisine is diverse and varies across different regions. It includes dishes like Peking duck, dim sum, stir-fries, dumplings, sweet and sour chicken, and fried rice.

Indian Cuisine:

Indian cuisine is characterized by its vibrant spices and rich flavors. It offers a wide variety of regional cuisines, such as North Indian (e.g., butter chicken, biryani), South Indian (e.g., dosa, idli), and Punjabi (e.g., tandoori chicken, naan).
